Getting set up

Topics

A topic is the title of your content. It can be anything from notes, to lists, to sketches. To enter a new topic, find the next available blank spread in your bullet journal. On the top left page, give your topic a brief descriptive title. Take a second here. Naming the topic can help you clarify and focus the content you're about to enter. If you anticipate this topic will be recurring, add a subtopic so it will be easier to find what you're looking for like "renovation / budget"

Page Numbers

Use as many pages as you need for your topic, but don’t worry about hoarding blank pages. Some recurring topics just won’t be consecutive, so it’s very important to always write down the page numbers in the bottom corners of each spread so you can quickly index all your topics.

Index

To index simply flip your notebook to it’s first blank page of your Bullet Journal’s. This page is called your smart index. It’s a combination of a traditional index page, and an appendix, but instead of chapters however, your index will list your topics. As you start filling your Bullet Journal, simply add your topics and their page numbers to the smart -index. Topics that span a series of page are indexed like this 5-10. Some topics however are recurring and can span multiple pages, spread out in your Bullet Journal. These topics can be indexed like this: Topic Name: 5-10, 23, 34-39 etc. If you used sub-topics, they will come in handy here: Topic / Subtopic / Page Number Subtopic / Page Number Subtopic / Page Number Indexing your topics provides you the flexibility to organically fill your Bullet Journal while still being able to quickly find your content.
